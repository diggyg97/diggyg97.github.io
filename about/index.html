I'm just trying to leverage data to answer policy questions.

Who am I?

My name is Digvijay Ghotane, but you can call me Diggy. I'm based in Washington D.C. for the foreseeable future, with home being the lovely city of Mumbai.

What do I do?

I'm currently in my final year of my graduate degree program at the McCourt School of Public Policy at Georgetown University. I will graduate in May 2021 with a Master of Science in Data Science for Public Policy.

I am interning at the World Resources Institute as a part of the climate team during the Summer of 2020, as a Data Science Intern. My role is to add to the Global Power Plant Database, that is a publicly available dataset for power plants around the world. In specific, I'm working on evaluating water use by power plants, essentially impacting energy policy alongside, environmental policy.

I've also worked in the past with Dr. Eric Dunford as his Research Assistant, and also currently work with Dr. C. Christine Fair as her Research Assistant, to assist with work with regard to security policy.

What did I do before?

I graduated with a Bachelor of Engineering from the University of Mumbai with the class of 2019. I was an Electronics and Telecommunication Engineering major. I also actively participated in Model United Nations conferences during my undergraduate years. I've also completed an online non-credit specialization in Business Analytics from the Wharton School of University of Pennsylvania in February 2019.

What do I want to do ahead?

Work toward the betterment of the world by solving problems by leveraging data.
